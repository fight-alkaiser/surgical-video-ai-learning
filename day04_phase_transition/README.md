Day04: Phase Transition Exploration

Objective

Investigate what happens around phase transition points in CholecT50 VID01.

Rather than focusing only on phase labels, examine the triplet annotations immediately before and after each transition.

Method

For each phase transition point, inspect the surrounding frames and display all active triplets.

Transitions analyzed:

* preparation → carlot-triangle-dissection
* carlot-triangle-dissection → clipping-and-cutting
* clipping-and-cutting → gallbladder-dissection
* gallbladder-dissection → gallbladder-packaging
* gallbladder-packaging → cleaning-and-coagulation
* cleaning-and-coagulation → gallbladder-extraction

Findings

Frame 22

The hook instrument appeared immediately before the transition to carlot-triangle-dissection.

Frame 674

The clipper instrument appeared at the transition to clipping-and-cutting.

Frame 888

The clipping phase ended and dissection activity resumed shortly after the transition.

Frame 1471

The packaging phase started before specimen bag manipulation became visible.

Frame 1569

The transition appeared to reflect a workflow change rather than the appearance of a new instrument.

Frame 1642

The extraction phase began after specimen bag manipulation had already started.

Observation

Some phase transitions coincide with the appearance of characteristic instruments.

However, several transitions occur before the defining action or instrument becomes visible.

This suggests that phase labels represent surgical workflow intent rather than discrete visual events.

Triplet annotations provide useful signals for understanding workflow transitions and may explain the recent shift from phase recognition toward event-based surgical video understanding.
