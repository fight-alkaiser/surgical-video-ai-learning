Observation

Several phase transitions were associated with the appearance of task-specific instruments or actions.

Examples include:

* clipper introduction before clipping-and-cutting
* specimen bag appearance before gallbladder packaging
* hook dissection activity before gallbladder dissection

However, not all phase transitions corresponded to newly appearing triplets.

The irrigator appeared much earlier than the cleaning-and-coagulation phase. From a surgical perspective, this is expected because irrigation and suction are frequently used throughout laparoscopic cholecystectomy for visualization, smoke removal, minor bleeding control, and field maintenance.

Therefore, the presence of an irrigator alone is not a reliable indicator of workflow phase.

These findings suggest that surgical workflow transitions are more closely related to task-specific events than to instrument presence alone.
